The problem

Crowdfunding still runs on blind trust.

The visitor's first question is not "What is Angor?" It is "Why does this need to exist?" The answer is simple: today's funding platforms make investors carry too much risk, founders suffer from trust friction, and platforms sit in the middle owning the relationship.

01

Money moves before confidence does.

Traditional platforms collect funds upfront, even though the real proof comes later. That creates a trust gap right when projects most need belief.

02

Backers have weak protection if momentum breaks.

When updates slow down or milestones slip, supporters are usually left with little recourse beyond frustration, comments, and crossed fingers.

03

Platforms become the center of the deal.

Intermediaries decide the rules, hold the rails, and often own the audience. That is the opposite of the open internet ethos many Bitcoin-native builders want.

The claim

Angor fixes crowdfunding by changing the agreement.

Instead of asking people to trust a founder, a platform, and a payment processor all at once, Angor uses Bitcoin-native contracts and decentralized communication to make accountability part of the funding model itself.

"The job of the platform is not to ask for more trust. It is to require less of it."

Objection handling

The next questions are obvious. So answer them directly.

A persuasive landing page should keep pace with the visitor's doubts. This section is designed to resolve the exact objections a first-time founder, investor, or founder-backed skeptic would raise after hearing the pitch.

"Is this just crowdfunding with crypto paint on it?"

No. Angor is not bolting tokens onto a traditional platform. It uses Bitcoin-native funding logic, milestone releases, and decentralized infrastructure to change the trust model itself.

"Will normal people understand it?"

They do not need to understand every protocol primitive. They only need to understand the value proposition: funds are structured, progress matters, and investors are not trapped.

"Why would a founder want more constraints?"

Because better constraints create more confidence. Angor helps serious founders look more credible by showing discipline before they ask for capital.

"What makes this feel trustworthy?"

Bitcoin-secured funding rails, staged release logic, decentralized communication via Nostr, and an open, protocol-led model that minimizes dependence on a central operator.

Deeper explanation

Under the hood, the story stays simple.

Once the visitor believes the concept, the next job is reinforcement. This part of the page explains how Angor works in enough detail to feel real, while staying accessible to people who are new to decentralized fundraising.

Launch a project with clear milestones.

Founders publish what they are building, how much bitcoin they want to raise, how the work is staged, and what progress unlocks the next release.

Backers fund through Bitcoin-native rails.

Capital is committed into a structure designed for staged delivery instead of one-way trust. That changes the feel of the investment immediately.

Updates and coordination happen over decentralized channels.

Angor uses Nostr for project metadata and communication so the relationship between founders and backers is not locked inside a central platform's database.

Progress earns funding. Stalls protect backers.

Milestone-based releases keep incentives aligned. If a project stops delivering, unspent funds remain recoverable, reducing the all-or-nothing risk.
